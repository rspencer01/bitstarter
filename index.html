About

Supercomputer.

The word inspires images of long rows of server racks stretching into the distance, blinking with thousands of red and green LEDs. The soft hum of air conditioning and the thousands of nodes working industiously away on the problems of humankind, or perhaps simply making sure that your friend request is sent to that person you met from a country half a world away.

This image is not wrong. But it is not the whole story either.

In fact, it is perfectly possible to build one at home. And that is the plan.

The definition of a "supercomputer" is simply any computer designed to have a high processing speed. One of the most common ways of doing this is "clustering": joining many computers together and distributing loads between them, and this is what we will impliment at .cmpt

Naturally, no project like this is without some costs, and while charges on computing time will keep the service above water once it is running, a project like this requires some initial capital. That is where you come in.

FAQ

How exactly will this work?
To use the .cmpt nodes, customers purchase computation (CPU) time. This is then spent on programs that customers send to the nodes to run. Once the program finishes its run, the output is then returned to the customer.
How fast will it be?

The answer to this is very dependant on the amount raised. The maths is simple. The more money raised, the better equipment we can purchase and the faster the result will be.

A typical, top of the range CPU will give up to 10 Giga-FLops. With a fairly small cluster of 10 CPUs, that will give 0.1 Tera-FLops. More will be faster. We aim to get about 1 TFLOP by the end of the first year.

An interesting possibility is presented by CPU PCI cards, such as Intel's Xeon Phi which sports a crazy 1 Tera-FLop on one card. Depending on avaliability, cost and installation difficulty, this remains a distinct possibility. Stay tuned!

How much will it cost?
The whole concept of .cmpt is to make high performance computing accesable. As such, node time will be accesibly cheap. While prices are not finalised yet, you can expect to pay about 0.00001 Bitcoin per second (or 1 Bitcoin = 100 000s = 1.16 days) of computation time.
Why use .cmpt over other supercomputer services?

Other supercomputer providers allow access to their computers for prices starting at $300 per hour. At the July 2013 bitcoin value, .cmpt will charge $3 per hour.

Granted, we will not have the same computing power as multi-national corperations, but as time progresses, the system will improve. This makes .cmpt perfect for individuals, small businesses and academics who require high performance computing without large cost.

So what do I get for bitstarting?
For your contribution you will receive time on the nodes at half the usual price.
